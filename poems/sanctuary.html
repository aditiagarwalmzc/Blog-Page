My Sanctuary, My Shrine

Hiding from the hideous humanity,
I enter my sanctuary, my shrine.
A room of stolen things and thoughts,
that once were never mine.

A holy ground with merry bells,
forbids no one but God.
Lies midway yet closer to hell
That rose a rebel, flawed.

A chamber full of quills and quests,
as if a rafflesia wearing Cologne.
This coffin brings in me what's best,
Yet I am always accused of being alone.

I, for a bit, sneaked outside,
discovered the routine humane hypocrisy.
Ran back to my sanctuary, my shrine,
hid away, safe and sound, from the hideous hideous humanity.
